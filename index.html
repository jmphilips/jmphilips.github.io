joshua philips developer




From a very early age, I enjoyed both creating things and technology. Beginning with Legos my love for building and creating grew to include custom furniture and applications.

Over time I realized that at the core of these interests was a passion for learning and problem solving which lead me to a career teaching English as a Second Language both domestically and internationally. While helping others to learn was very rewarding, I found it somewhat limiting when it came to expanding my own knowledge, so I began spending more and more time outside of work learning to program.

After completing all I could online learning HTML and CSS I decided to give Ruby a try and it was love at first gem. I remember falling in love and falling asleep at the command line in my early days of learning how to program. Even though I was only making small apps that did things like conjugate Japanese verbs, using technology to build things felt like something I had been preparing for all along.

The time had come for me to take steps to pursue the career that I was always meant to have and in May 2016 I was accepted to Nashville Software School as a part of the Cohort 14 bootcamp working with the MEAN stack. Although it was not always clear, I have slowly been becoming a developer since the first time I combined two different sets of legos to create something new. Now, with the skills that I have learned through NSS, I can’t wait to build real solutions for real problems and continue to learn for years to come.
